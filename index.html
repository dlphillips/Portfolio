About Me

I'm originally from Louisville, Kentucky. My undergraduate education was fueled by a passion for weather and maps. This led to an Associates Degree in Meteorology from Western Kentucky University and was followed by a Bachelors Degree in Geography from the University of Louisville. There, I focused my studies on an emerging computer technology: Geographic Information Systems.

My career thus far has been very fulfilling. It has allowed me to work daily assisting clients throughout the US & Canada. Automating complex procedures through either database or GIS technologies is what I have found to be most rewarding. If you have ordered a pizza over the last 20 years, it is likely that I helped the food arrive hot at your door. This is because my company supplies street and other map information for point of sale systems. Processes to automate the creation and formatting of this data were authored by myself and fellow team members. Delivery wall maps that were automated through processes I streamlined are hanging on the walls of thousands of restaurants.

Throughout my career, it seems a strong driving factor for me is to help others make well-informed decisions. It is extremely satisfying when you can provide an “AH-HA!” moment for someone through clear, engaging presentation of data. I see web development as a natural progression that will allow me to provide similar insights in new, innovative ways. With every new challenge that as presented, I have no doubt I have chosen the correct path for my future.

On the personal side, I’ve always enjoyed the outdoors with activities that include SCUBA, golf and camping. I volunteered with the Boy Scouts of America in Louisville in several troop positions and I’m proud to say I completed Wood Badge training; the highest and most advanced leadership training course offered by the Boy Scouts of America. I’ve lived in Charlotte since July 2014 and fallen in love with the city. Currently, my free time is spent with family and serving in my church.
